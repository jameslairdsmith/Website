--- title: "About me" author: "Eric Ekholm" date: "12/27/2019" ---

I am an educator, researcher, data scientist, and writer. I am currently the Associate Director of Early Childhood Program Performance at the Virginia Department of Education, where I work to expand access to and improve the quality of early childhood education programs across the state of Virginia. In my free time, I enjoy reading, writing, data visualization/modeling, going to the gym, and spending time with friends & family (including my pup, Nala).


I earned my PhD in educational psychology from the VCU School of Education in July 2019, where worked in the Motivation and Self-Regulation Lab and in the Cognition and Learning Lab to study students’ motivation, behaviors, and achievement. In my dissertation, I studied how writers’ daily emotional states (think: anxiety, enjoyment, boredom) influenced their productivity as well as the day-to-day inertia of these emotional states.

I typically use R to wrangle (e.g. {tidyverse}), model (e.g. {tidymodels}, {lme4}, {lavaan}), and visualize (e.g. {ggplot2}, {shiny}) data, and I am deeply interested in how to effectively translate insights from data into practice.
